What We Found

The Problems — Ranked by Impact

Critical
{{BUSINESS_NAME}} has two live websites saying different things
Screenshot needed: Side-by-side of {{OLD_DOMAIN}} and {{NEW_DOMAIN}} showing conflicting information

Your old site at {{OLD_DOMAIN}} is still live on the internet with the same phone number as your new site. It lists different service areas and different information. Google and AI see both sites, compare them, and get confused. When two sources disagree, AI systems trust neither.

This is the most urgent fix. Until it's resolved, every other improvement you make is undermined.

Critical
Google can't read who you are or where you operate

Your website has no schema markup — the structured code that tells Google and AI your exact business name, address, phone, service area, and what services you offer. Without it, Google has to guess. Businesses with schema get recommended; businesses without it get skipped.

No schema means no rich results in search, no confident AI recommendations, and no structured signal telling Google which towns you serve.

High Impact
Location pages that don't actually work for SEO

{{LOCATION_PAGE_ISSUE_DESCRIPTION}}

Google detects duplicate content across pages and discounts those pages in rankings. Each town you serve needs its own unique page with content specific to that location — not a copy with the city name swapped.

High Impact
No meta descriptions on any page

Every page on {{WEBSITE_URL}} is missing a meta description — the short summary that appears under your site in Google search results. Google writes one for you when it's missing, and it's rarely good. A well-written meta description for "{{SERVICE_TYPE}} {{CITY}} SC" is one of the cheapest clicks you can earn — and right now you're leaving it to chance.

Medium
No real contact page

There's no dedicated contact page with your hours, service area in plain language, email address, and what a customer should expect when they call. A customer who lands on your site and can't find basic business information doesn't call — they click back and find someone else.

Medium
No FAQ schema — your answers aren't showing up in search

You have a FAQ page, but the questions and answers aren't marked up with FAQPage schema. That markup is what allows Google to display your answers directly in search results — above the regular links. Businesses with FAQ schema get more real estate on the results page and more clicks without paying for ads.
